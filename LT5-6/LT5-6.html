Consider the following strategies:
I will choose Not first, as long as my accomplice
chooses NC, I will stick to it;

however, if I observe that after certain periods,
my accomplice chooses C, I will choose C forever.
There are cooperative equilibria.
A bit Math...
Assuming discount rate  $\delta = 0.8$
In the first period, I deviate, I get 0

immediately, but -8 forever...
$0 +0.8*(-8)+0.8^2*(-8) +... $
my profit $\pi = 0.8 * \frac{-8}{1-0.8} = -32 $
If I cooperate, I get -0.1 forever...
my profit is $\pi = \frac{-0.1}{1-0.8} = -0.5$
No incentive to confess
What makes the difference?
